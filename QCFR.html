Quality Control File Reviews and Annual Compliance Reviews

Cold File Reviews
(Quality Control File Reviews)

Cold file reviews are undertaken after the assignment has been completed.

They cover a review of the following:

  • Financial statements
  • Permanent File
  • The Review and Finalisation Procedures
  • All audit areas that are above performance materiality
  • Any immaterial areas (if you request it)

They are a vital part of your practice’s on-going quality control procedures, they are also sometimes a specific requirement of your Regulator.

Metis Compliance Limited can undertake cold reviews on all types of audit assignments to allow you to comply with ISQC1 and ISA 220 or any requirement imposed by your regulator. We can also undertake cold file reviews on assurance files, ‘client money audits’ or accountancy assignments.

Whilst these reviews are not usually time sensitive, we would try wherever possible to meet your deadlines and would aim to undertake them within 5 working days of a request being made.

Please note it is not appropriate for Metis to undertake a cold file review where we have previously undertaken a hot file review, an Engagement Quality Control Review, where we have offered a mentoring service or where we have given significant technical advice.

Engagement Quality Control Review (EQCR) and Hot File Reviews

These reviews are undertaken before the audit partner signs the audit report.

Hot file reviews are similar in nature to cold file reviews, there are two key considerations, to get the most out of them the audit file needs to be as finalised as it can be, also time needs to be allowed for the findings of the hot file review to be appropriately actioned prior to signing the audit report.

An Engagement Quality Control Review (EQCR) are effectively a ‘focused’ hot file review which concentrates on significant judgements made and conclusions reached in formulating the auditor’s report. For example an EQCR may be limited to reviewing the going concern status of the client or reviewing whether adequate consideration has been given to an ethical issue. These type of reviews may be a response to risk, to comply with the requirements of the FRC’s Ethical Standard or the requirements of a regulator. It will usually be appropriate for the review to happen in two stages, a review of the planning, then a second review just before the assignment is finalised.

These types of reviews can also be taken on other types of assurance engagement and accountancy files.

At the end of each review, we will discuss our findings with you and issue you with a report which documents our findings, recommendations and conclusion.

We appreciate that this type of review is very time sensitive and will make every effort to meet your deadlines.

Efficiency Reviews

Whilst inefficiencies will be noted during both hot and cold file reviews, you might as a practice have concerns about why you generally or in relation to a specific client attaining unsatisfactory recovery rates. We can undertake a detailed review in a free format discussing the potential inefficiencies that we have identified.

Annual Compliance Reviews – Anti-Money Laundering

Failure to comply with the Money Laundering Regulations is probably the biggest regularity threat that practicing accountants face.

Metis’ Managing Director has a vast wealth of experience in the field of Anti-Money Laundering, having written anti-money laundering manuals, and having also worked for an IFAC Accountancy Regulator Body undertaking Anti-Money Laundering Compliance Reviews.

It is mandatory to undertake an annual anti-money laundering compliance review, failure to do so is a criminal offence. Whether your regulator requires that this is undertaken by an external organisation or whether you feel it would just be more beneficial to have it undertaken externally, Metis can undertake this review for you.

Also, unless you are a sole practitioner with no staff or subcontractors then there is also a requirement for this annual compliance review to be ‘audited’ again Metis can help you with this.

Annual Compliance Review – Client Money

This is an area of regulatory risk.

Every practice who holds client money should undertake a client money annual compliance review. Whether your regulator requires that this is undertaken by an external organisation or whether you feel it would just be more beneficial to have it undertaken externally, Metis can undertake the review for you.

Annual Compliance Reviews – DPB

If your practice holds a Designated Professional Body (Investment Business) licence then a DPB annual compliance review should be undertaken, even where you do not believe that you undertake any investment business. Whether your regulator requires that this is undertaken by an external organisation or whether you feel it would just be more beneficial to have it undertaken externally, Metis can undertake this review for you.

Annual Compliance Review – Audit

If your practice is a practice of registered auditors then an audit annual compliance review should be undertaken. Whether your regulator requires that this is undertaken by an external organisation or whether you feel it would just be more beneficial to have it undertaken externally, Metis can undertake this review for you. This works well if we also undertake cold file reviews for your practice.

Annual Compliance Review – Whole Practice Review

This review covers the following areas, unless they are not relevant or you have undertaken the review for yourself:

  • Anti-Money Laundering
  • Client Money
  • DBP
  • Audit
  • ‘Practice Assurance’ this is wide ranging but covers your marketing material, website, letterhead, compliance with a wide range a regulations. It also involves a review of a sample of non audit files. The review of non audit files is to ensure that your procedures have been complied with and they are adequate. Regarding direct taxation and VAT files the review will not considered whether treatments or advice are correct..
